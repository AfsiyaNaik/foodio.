Recipe 7: Gobi Manchurian


Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Serving: 4 servings

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornflour (cornstarch)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed to make batter
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for the sauce)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons water
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the cauliflower by blanching the florets in boiling water for 3-4 minutes. Drain and set aside to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Add water little by little to make a smooth batter.
  3. Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter and coat evenly.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Fry the battered cauliflower florets in batches until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. For the sauce, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and sauté chopped onions, bell peppers, and green chilies until they soften.
  6. Add soy sauce, vinegar, ketchup, and sugar. Stir well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add 2-3 tablespoons of water to adjust the consistency of the sauce if needed.
  8. Gently toss the fried cauliflower florets in the sauce, making sure they are coated well.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice or as a snack. Enjoy your Gobi Manchurian!
